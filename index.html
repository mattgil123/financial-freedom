Build Wealth

Your Investing Playbook

📊
The Golden Rule
Invest 15–20% of your household income for retirement. If your employer matches 401k contributions, max it out — it's an instant 100% return on that money.
15%
🏦
Roth IRA — Pay Taxes Now

Since you are young, go with the Roth IRA. Pay the taxes now — growth and profit will be completely tax-free going forward.

💼
401k — Free Money Alert

If your employer matches contributions, maxing your 401k is like getting an immediate guaranteed return. Do not leave that on the table.

🚀
Go Aggressive (For Now)

Concentrate in high-growth sectors while young. As you get older, shift to target-date funds.

  • Technology
  • Artificial Intelligence
  • Robotics (very aggressive — know what you're doing)
  • Healthcare
🎯
Keep It Simple: Index Funds

Broad index funds are your best friend. Diversified, low-cost, proven.

Vanguard 500 VTI — Total Market QQQ — Nasdaq 100

QQQ is tech-heavy but powerful. Buy individual stocks only after all of the above is in place.

Financial Hygiene

Other Tips That Matter

🏠
Don't Become House Poor
The rules have changed. A house is no longer just an asset — in many ways, it is a liability. Factor in taxes, maintenance, and opportunity cost before buying.
📉
Minimize Depreciating Assets
Cars, electronics, and luxury goods lose value the moment you buy them. Keep these acquisitions to a minimum.
💸
Avoid Lifestyle Inflation

Hedonic adaptation is real — and very middle class. Every raise is a wealth-building opportunity, not a spending permission slip.

🔒
Keep Your Assets Secure
  • Loose lips sink ships — there are sharks everywhere
  • Encrypt everything sensitive
  • Use passphrases, not passwords
  • Use Secure ID tokens or SSO when possible
🧠
Build Your Talent Stack
A talent stack is combining multiple complementary skills to create a unique, highly valuable professional profile — rather than being the absolute best at one highly competitive discipline.
🎲
Have Fun — Responsibly
After all your bases are covered, invest in some fun and "Hail Mary" companies from time to time. Life is meant to be enjoyed, not just optimized.
